East Side Fish Market
44 Dockside Wharf
Wooden crates filled with melting ice and fresh catch line the damp brick walkways. The heavy, metallic smell of salt water and raw fish hangs frozen in the cold night breeze sweeping off the harbor.
ENVIRONMENT

A few merchants move under the light of hand-held flashlights, wrapping the remaining inventory in wet newspapers. The sudden blackout has ruined their morning sales schedule, and the mood is bitter. Nobody remembers seeing anyone carrying unusual equipment or heavy cargo through the docks.

The scales and cutting tables show no traces of luminescent particles—only salt and fish scales. The suspect bypassed these docks entirely when moving his payload. This is just another ordinary harbor front losing its livelihood in the dark.
